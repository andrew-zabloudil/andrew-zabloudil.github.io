My Story

My first experience with programming was in middle school. I begged my parents to buy a book for me and I taught myself HTML from it. I was instantly hooked from that day on. In high school I took my school's only two programming courses covering Visual Basic and Java, but had no direction to continue after completing them which led me to take a break from programming.

I entered college two years later and, chasing after a desire to have a career with lots of problem solving, obtained a degree in Mechanical Engineering. Even throughout college, though, my love for programming surfaced in a select few classes which used an Arduino. I began learning Japanese on my own during college and also found myself enamored with language learning.

After graduating with my degree in Mechanical Engineering, my desire to be a problem solver and love of languages eventually led me to rediscover programming again. With the realization that programming perfectly combines the things I loved about engineering and language learning, I picked up Python and haven't looked back since.
